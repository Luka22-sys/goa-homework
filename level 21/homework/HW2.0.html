lion family

Lion Family and Social Structure: Pride: A lion pride typically consists of several related females (lionesses), their cubs, and a few adult males. The females are usually related to each other, and they cooperate in hunting and raising cubs. Males typically stay with the pride for a few years before being replaced by new males who challenge for dominance. Roles in the Pride: Lionesses: They do most of the hunting and caregiving. Lionesses work together to hunt in coordinated strategies, and they share the responsibility of protecting the young. Males: Male lions primarily defend the pride's territory from other males. They often protect the pride from external threats and help in controlling other predators. Cubs: Cubs are born blind and dependent on the mother. Lionesses will sometimes nurse and care for each other's cubs, creating a strong communal bond among females. Cubs are weaned around 6-7 months old but stay with the pride for up to two years or more. Lions are found in sub-Saharan Africa and a small population in the Gir Forest of India (the Asiatic lion).
